About the Dataset

The dataset consists of thousands of labeled images representing various common skin diseases. It is designed to train and test AI models for accurate skin disease classification, supporting early detection and awareness. Each image is categorized by disease type, ensuring diverse representation for robust model learning.

Disease Descriptions & Treatments

Cellulitis

A bacterial skin infection causing redness, swelling, and pain. Treatment involves oral or intravenous antibiotics. Early intervention prevents complications.

Impetigo

A highly contagious bacterial infection, often in children, causing red sores. Treated with topical or oral antibiotics. Good hygiene helps prevent spread.

Athleteâ€™s Foot

A fungal infection between the toes, causing itching and scaling. Treated with antifungal creams or oral medication. Keeping feet dry helps prevent recurrence.

Nail Fungus

A fungal infection of the nails, leading to discoloration and thickening. Treated with antifungal nail lacquers, oral antifungals, or laser therapy.

Ringworm

A contagious fungal infection forming a ring-shaped rash. Treated with topical or oral antifungal medications. Personal hygiene is crucial for prevention.

Cutaneous Larva Migrans

A skin infection caused by hookworm larvae, resulting in itchy, winding rash. Treated with antiparasitic medications like albendazole or ivermectin.

Chickenpox

A viral infection causing itchy blisters and fever. Usually self-limiting; calamine lotion and antihistamines relieve symptoms. Antivirals for severe cases.

Shingles

A reactivation of the chickenpox virus, causing painful rash. Treated with antiviral drugs (acyclovir, valacyclovir) and pain relievers. Vaccination helps prevent it.
